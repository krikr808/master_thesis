\chapter{Finite volume method}

The \FVM is a way to realistically simulate the flow of a fluid by letting the fluid flow between a large number of adjactent \idxp{cell}{s}, through the \idxp{cell face}{s}. The magnitude of the \idxs{fluid}{flux} between two cells is directly proportional to the area of the cell face and the component of the velocity of the fluid in the direction of the cell face normal.

When simulating fluids with the \FVM it is common to \idxe{approximation}{approximate} the fluid as being \idxse{incompressible}{fluid}{incompressible}. This is because it is difficult to simulate compressible fluids when the \idx{time step} becomes large, due to \idxe{instability}{instabilities} in the \idx{numerical method} which leads to \idxs{spurious}{oscillations}.
